Classes of Food

Carbohydrate

Carbohydrates are the bodyâ€™s primary source of energy, supplying the fuel needed for daily activities, brain function, and physical movement. They are broken down into glucose, which the body uses immediately or stores for later use.

  • Yam
  • Cassava
  • Bread
  • Plantain
  • Potato

Protein

Proteins are essential for growth, repair, and maintenance of body tissues. They are made up of amino acids, which are the building blocks of muscles, enzymes, hormones, and other vital components of the body.

  • Eggs
  • Fish
  • Chicken
  • Beans
  • Nuts

Vitamin

Vitamins are organic compounds that are crucial for various bodily functions, including immune support, energy production, and bone health. They are typically obtained from a balanced diet rich in fruits and vegetables.

  • Carrot
  • Orange
  • Apple
  • Tomato
  • Mango

Fat

Fats are a concentrated source of energy and are essential for the absorption of fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, and K). They also play a role in protecting organs, insulating the body, and supporting cell growth.

  • Avocado
  • Olive oil
  • Yoghurt
  • Butter
  • Cheese

Mineral

Minerals are inorganic nutrients that are vital for various bodily functions, including bone formation, nerve transmission, and muscle contraction. They are obtained from a variety of foods, including dairy products, meats, and vegetables.

  • Milk
  • Red meat
  • Salt
  • Spinach
  • Broccoli

Fibre

Dietary fibre is important for digestive health, helping to regulate bowel movements and prevent constipation. It also aids in maintaining healthy cholesterol levels and blood sugar control.

  • Millet
  • Vegetables
  • Fruits
  • Legumes
  • Oats

Water

Water is essential for life, making up a significant portion of the human body. It is crucial for maintaining hydration, regulating body temperature, and facilitating various physiological processes, including digestion and nutrient transport.

  • Plain water
  • Herbal teas
  • Fruits with high water content (e.g., watermelon, cucumber)
